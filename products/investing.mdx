## How It Works

When you invest in an Axis ETF, you swap a token (typically SOL) into the ETF pool. In return, you receive an **ETF token** — an SPL token representing your proportional share of the underlying basket.

This token:
- Is standard SPL — transferable, composable, tradeable
- Represents a claim on the underlying assets
- Can be redeemed at any time for the underlying tokens

---

## Investing

1. Open an ETF from the Discover feed
2. Tap **Invest**
3. Enter the amount you want to swap in
4. Review the estimated output and slippage
5. Confirm the transaction in your wallet

Your swap request enters the PFDA batch queue. It will be cleared within the next batch window (~10 Solana slots, approximately 4 seconds). After clearing, your ETF tokens are available.

<Note>
Because of the PFDA batch mechanism, your transaction has two steps: the initial swap request, and the Claim step after the batch clears. The Claim is initiated automatically in the UI — you may need to sign a second transaction.
</Note>

---

## Redeeming

1. Go to **Portfolio**
2. Find your ETF position
3. Tap **Redeem**
4. Enter the amount of ETF tokens to redeem
5. Confirm the transaction

You will receive the underlying tokens proportionally based on the current basket composition. The redemption also goes through the PFDA batch queue.

---

## Understanding Your Position

The ETF token price is derived from the underlying basket value — it changes as the prices of the underlying tokens change.

Your ETF token is worth:
```
(your ETF tokens / total ETF supply) × total basket value
```

---

## Slippage

Slippage depends on pool liquidity and basket composition.

| Environment | Slippage behavior |
|---|---|
| **Devnet** | Liquidity is thin. Slippage may be significant for all but the smallest trades. |
| **Mainnet** | Routes through Jupiter. Depends on underlying token liquidity. |

You can set a slippage tolerance before confirming. The PFDA mechanism enforces `min_amount_out` at the Claim step — if the clearing price falls outside your tolerance, the transaction will fail and your tokens will be returned.

---

## The PFDA and Invest Timing

Your swap request is not executed immediately. It is queued in a batch for up to 10 slots. This is a deliberate design choice: by batching orders and clearing at a single price, Axis prevents front-running and MEV extraction on your trade.

In practice, the delay is imperceptible (~4 seconds). The UI handles the queue and Claim flow automatically.
