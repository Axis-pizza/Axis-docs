Anyone can create an on-chain ETF on Axis. No approval, no minimum deposit, no KYC.

---

## What You're Creating

An Axis ETF consists of:

- A **basket** of 2–10 SPL tokens with target weights (must sum to 100%)
- A **vault** that holds the underlying assets
- An **SPL token mint** representing proportional shares
- A **pool** connected to the PFDA batch auction for rebalancing

Once deployed, the ETF is live on-chain permanently. Anyone can discover and invest in it.

---

## Creation Flow

1. Connect your wallet
2. Tap **Create** in the navigation
3. Fill in:
   - **Name** — displayed on the ETF card (e.g. "Solana Blue Chips")
   - **Ticker** — short identifier (e.g. "SBLUE")
   - **Description** — optional, shown on detail page
   - **Tokens + Weights** — add tokens, assign % weights (must total 100%)
4. Tap **Create ETF** and confirm in your wallet

One transaction. Your ETF is live.

---

## Token Selection

**Devnet:** Any SPL token available on Solana Devnet.

**Mainnet:** Any SPL token with sufficient liquidity for Jupiter to route swaps. Tokens without tradeable pairs will not function correctly for invest/redeem flows.

Long-tail tokens with thin liquidity will show higher slippage for investors. See [The Long-Tail Asset Problem](/research/long-tail-assets).

---

## Weight Configuration

Weights represent the target allocation of the basket. Example:

| Token | Weight |
|---|---|
| SOL | 50% |
| JUP | 30% |
| BONK | 20% |

Weights are fixed at creation. The protocol handles rebalancing to maintain these weights as prices drift. See [Rebalancing](/products/rebalancing).

---

## After Creation

Your ETF appears in the Discover feed immediately. You can:

- Share the ETF URL
- Track TVL and volume in your Portfolio view

You cannot modify the basket or weights after creation.

---

## Devnet vs. Mainnet

The creation flow is identical on both environments. The difference is that Devnet uses no real funds and the PFDA mechanism is simulated rather than live.
