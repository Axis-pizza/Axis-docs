**URL:** [axs.pizza](https://axs.pizza)  
**Network:** Solana Devnet  
**Access:** Open — anyone can use it  
**Funds:** No real money. Devnet SOL only.

The Devnet is the best place to explore Axis with no risk. You can create ETFs, invest in others, and experience the full product flow using Devnet tokens.

---

## Before You Start

You need a Solana wallet. Axis supports:

- [Phantom](https://phantom.app)
- [Solflare](https://solflare.com)
- [Backpack](https://backpack.app)

Make sure your wallet is set to **Devnet** before connecting.

In Phantom: Settings → Developer Settings → Change Network → Devnet

---

## Get Devnet SOL

You need Devnet SOL to pay for transactions. Get it free from a faucet:

- [faucet.solana.com](https://faucet.solana.com) — paste your wallet address and request SOL
- Or run `solana airdrop 2` if you have the Solana CLI installed

2–4 SOL is enough to test everything.

---

## Discover ETFs

The main screen shows ETFs created by other users. Each card displays:

- ETF name and ticker
- Underlying basket composition
- Total Value Locked (TVL)

Tap any card to see the full detail view.

---

## Invest in an ETF

1. Open an ETF from the Discover feed
2. Tap **Invest**
3. Enter the amount of SOL (or supported token) you want to swap in
4. Confirm the transaction in your wallet

You will receive ETF tokens representing your proportional share of the basket. These are standard SPL tokens — you can hold, transfer, or redeem them at any time.

To exit, tap **Redeem** on your position and confirm the transaction. You will receive the underlying tokens back proportionally.

---

## Create an ETF

1. Tap **Create** in the navigation
2. Fill in the details:
   - **Name** — e.g. "Solana Blue Chips"
   - **Ticker** — e.g. "SBLUE"
   - **Description** — optional, shown on the ETF card
   - **Tokens** — add 2–10 SPL tokens and set target weights (must sum to 100%)
3. Tap **Create ETF** and confirm the transaction

Your ETF is now live on Devnet. Anyone can discover and invest in it.

<Tip>
On Devnet, you can create ETFs with any SPL token. The experience is identical to Mainnet except funds are not real.
</Tip>

## Limitations of the Devnet

The Devnet MVP runs the full product flow but has some limitations:

- Liquidity is thin — large trades will show high slippage
- The PFDA batch auction is active but the Jito searcher network is not running on Devnet; batch clearing is simulated
- Some long-tail tokens may not have Devnet liquidity

For a full production experience with the PFDA mechanism running end-to-end, see [Mainnet — Closed Beta](/getting-started/mainnet).
