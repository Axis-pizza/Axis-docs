## General

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What is Axis?">
    Axis is a permissionless on-chain ETF platform on Solana. Anyone can create a basket of crypto tokens as a tradeable ETF, and anyone can invest in one with a single transaction.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Is Axis a DEX?">
    Not exactly. Axis is an ETF protocol — the underlying swap routing uses Jupiter, but the core product is basket creation, investment, and rebalancing. The PFDA mechanism handles rebalancing in a fundamentally different way from a standard AMM.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Do I need KYC to use Axis?">
    No. The protocol is permissionless and non-custodial. You connect a wallet and transact directly on-chain. No account creation or identity verification is required. U.S. persons are excluded from Mainnet by geographic restriction.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Who controls my funds?">
    Nobody. Your assets are held in smart contract vaults on Solana. Axis, Inc. has no custody over user funds. You can withdraw at any time by calling Redeem.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Devnet vs. Mainnet

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What's the difference between Devnet and Mainnet?">
    Devnet ([axs.pizza](https://axs.pizza)) runs on Solana Devnet with no real funds. It's open to anyone. Mainnet ([mainnet.axs.pizza](https://mainnet.axs.pizza)) runs on Solana Mainnet with real funds and is invite-only during closed beta.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I use the same wallet on both?">
    Yes. Your Solana wallet works on both networks. Just switch your wallet's network setting when switching between Devnet and Mainnet.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Will my Devnet ETFs carry over to Mainnet?">
    No. Devnet and Mainnet are entirely separate environments. Devnet ETFs, positions, and history are not transferred.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## ETF Creation

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What tokens can I include in an ETF basket?">
    Any SPL token supported by Jupiter routing. On Devnet, you can use any available Devnet token. On Mainnet, tokens need sufficient liquidity for Jupiter to route swaps.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How many tokens can a basket contain?">
    Between 2 and 10 tokens.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I change the basket composition after creation?">
    No. The token composition and weights are fixed at creation. Only the dynamic weight rebalancing (handled by the protocol) adjusts actual holdings over time.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Is there a cost to create an ETF?">
    Only the Solana transaction fee (typically less than 0.001 SOL). There is no protocol fee for creation.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Investing

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What do I receive when I invest in an ETF?">
    You receive an SPL token representing your proportional share of the ETF's underlying basket. This token is transferable and can be redeemed at any time.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I exit a position?">
    Tap Redeem on your position in the Portfolio view. You will receive the underlying tokens proportionally.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Is there slippage?">
    Yes. On Devnet, liquidity is thin and slippage may be significant for larger trades. On Mainnet, routing goes through Jupiter and slippage depends on underlying token liquidity.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What is the PFDA and how does it affect my swap?">
    Your swap request is queued in a batch for up to 10 Solana slots (~4 seconds). At the end of the window, all requests in the batch are cleared at a single uniform price. This means you cannot be front-run. After clearing, you call Claim to receive your output tokens. See [PFDA Mechanism](/protocol/pfda).
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Fees

<AccordionGroup>

## Security

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Has Axis been audited?">
    Not yet. The protocol is unaudited as of the Mainnet closed beta. A Tier 2/3 audit is planned before Mainnet V1. Do not deposit funds you cannot afford to lose.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What are the main risks?">
    Smart contract risk (unaudited), oracle risk (Switchboard dependency), liquidity risk (thin pools on Devnet), and design risk (architecture still being finalized). See [Known Risks](/trust/risks).
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
